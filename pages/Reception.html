Box office Reception

The Star Wars prequel trilogy was a box office success, with each film earning millions of dollars worldwide.

  • Phantom Menace: $431,088,295 to date
  • Attack of the Clones: $302,191,252 to date
  • Revenge of the Sith: 380,270,577 to date


Critical Reception

Overall, while the prequels were initially met with mixed to negative reviews, they have gained a more positive reassessment from some fans and critics over the years.

  • The Phantom Menace (1999): Initially, it received mixed reviews. Critics praised its visual effects and action sequences but criticized its story and characters. Over time, its Rotten Tomatoes score has dropped from 62% to 54%
  • Attack of the Clones (2002): This installment also had mixed reviews, with some appreciation for its action and special effects but criticism for its dialogue and pacing. Its score has remained relatively stable, hovering around 65%
  • Revenge of the Sith (2005): This film was the most well-received of the prequels, with an initial score of 83% that later settled around 79%. It was praised for its darker tone and emotional depth
